Some random information.

Neck and Legs: Despite their long necks, giraffes actually have the same number of neck vertebrae as humans-seven!
However, each vertebra is much longer. Their legs are also incredibly long, with each leg measuring about 6 feet (1.8 meters).

Savannas are not purely grasslands or forests; they are a mixture of both.
The landscape is characterized by vast stretches of grass with some scattered trees and shrubs,
typically acacias or baobabs, which are adapted to the dry conditions.

Lions have one of the most powerful roars in the animal kingdom.
Their roars can be heard from up to 5 miles (8 kilometers) away and serve to communicate with other lions,
establish territory, and warn off potential threats. A lion's roar can reach up to 114 decibels, which is louder than a chainsaw.

Elephants communicate through a wide range of sounds, some of which are infrasonic (below the human hearing range).
These low-frequency rumbles can travel over long distances, allowing elephants to communicate with each other even if they are miles apart.
They also use body language, such as ear movements, trunk gestures, and even their sense of smell to express themselves.
